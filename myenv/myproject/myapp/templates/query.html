What's the difference between third-party and comprehensive insurance? +
Third-party insurance only covers damages to other people or their property caused by your vehicle (legally mandatory). Comprehensive insurance covers third-party liabilities PLUS damages to your own vehicle due to accidents, theft, fire, natural disasters, etc. While third-party is cheaper, comprehensive provides complete protection and is highly recommended despite costing 2-3 times more.
Does the policy cover accidents, theft, and natural calamities? +
Comprehensive vehicle insurance covers all these scenarios. It provides protection against accidental damage, theft, fire, explosion, self-ignition, lightning, floods, earthquakes, landslides, rockslides, and other natural calamities. Third-party insurance, however, only covers damages to third parties and doesn't protect your vehicle against these risks.
What is IDV (Insured Declared Value) and how is it calculated? +
IDV (Insured Declared Value) is the maximum amount payable if your vehicle is stolen or suffers total loss. It's calculated as: Manufacturer's listed selling price - depreciation based on vehicle age. Depreciation rates: <6 months: 5%, 6-12 months: 15%, 1-2 years: 20%, 2-3 years: 30%, 3-4 years: 40%, 4-5 years: 50%, >5 years: negotiated value. Higher IDV means higher premium and vice versa.
Can I transfer my No Claim Bonus (NCB) to a new insurer? +
Yes, you can transfer your No Claim Bonus (NCB) to a new insurer or a new vehicle. NCB is a reward for claim-free years, offering discounts from 20% (after 1 claim-free year) up to 50% (after 5+ claim-free years). To transfer NCB, request an NCB certificate from your current insurer before policy expiry and submit it to the new insurer within 90 days.
How many claims can I make in a year? +
There's no fixed limit on the number of claims you can make in a policy year, but each claim affects your No Claim Bonus (NCB). Even a single claim will reset your NCB to zero. For minor damages, consider paying out of pocket to preserve your NCB. Some premium plans offer "NCB Protect" add-on that allows one claim per year without affecting your NCB.
Is roadside assistance or zero depreciation included? +
Base policies typically don't include these benefits, but they can be added as riders. Roadside assistance covers services like towing, battery jump-start, flat tire change, etc., costing â‚¹500-â‚¹1,500/year. Zero depreciation (or bumper-to-bumper) coverage eliminates depreciation deductions during claims, costing 10-20% extra premium, but is highly recommended for new vehicles up to 5 years old.

What is the term of the policy? +
Our life insurance policies offer flexible terms: Term plans range from 5-40 years or up to age 75-85. Whole life plans provide coverage until age 99-100. Endowment/ULIP plans typically offer 10-30 year terms. We recommend choosing a term that covers your productive years (typically until retirement) or matches specific financial goals like children's education or mortgage duration.
How much sum assured should I choose? +
A general guideline is 10-15 times your annual income. Consider your financial obligations (loans, mortgage), future expenses (children's education), and income replacement needs. Our advisors recommend calculating based on the "Human Life Value" method that factors in your income, expenses, liabilities, inflation, and dependents. Use our online calculator for a personalized recommendation.
What happens if I miss a premium payment? +
Most policies offer a grace period of 15-30 days during which you can pay without penalties. If payment isn't made within this period, the policy may lapse. For policies that have acquired surrender value (typically after 2-3 years), they become paid-up with reduced benefits. Lapsed policies can be revived within 2-5 years by paying outstanding premiums plus interest.
Will my nominee face any issues during claim settlement? +
We ensure a hassle-free claim process with minimal documentation. To prevent issues: 1) Disclose all medical conditions during application, 2) Keep premiums up-to-date, 3) Inform nominees about the policy, 4) Keep policy documents accessible, 5) Update beneficiary details regularly. Claims are typically settled within 7 days for non-investigative cases if all documents are in order.
What's the difference between term life, whole life, and endowment plans? +
Term life provides pure protection for a specific period with no maturity benefits but offers the highest coverage at lowest cost. Whole life provides lifelong protection (typically until age 100) with savings component and some cash value. Endowment plans offer lower coverage but guaranteed returns at maturity, combining protection and savings with typically higher premiums than term plans.
Is there a maturity benefit if I survive the policy term? +
This depends on the policy type. Traditional term insurance has no maturity benefit. Return of Premium (TROP) plans return all premiums paid if you survive the term. Endowment/savings plans offer guaranteed maturity amounts plus bonuses. ULIPs provide fund value at maturity. Whole life plans typically pay the sum assured plus bonuses upon reaching a certain age (usually 100).
Can I take a loan against this policy? +
Loans are available against permanent life insurance policies (whole life, endowment, ULIPs) that have acquired surrender value, typically after 3 years. You can borrow up to 75-90% of the surrender value at interest rates usually 1-2% above the policy's guaranteed rate. Term insurance policies do not offer loan facilities as they don't accumulate cash value.
