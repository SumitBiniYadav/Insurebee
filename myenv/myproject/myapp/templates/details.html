Product Description

With level premiums throughout the duration of your policy, you'll never have to worry about unexpected price increases. This predictability makes it easier to budget for your insurance needs while maintaining comprehensive coverage.

One of the standout features of this policy is the option to convert to a permanent life insurance plan without requiring a new medical examination. This flexibility allows you to adapt your coverage as your needs change over time.

The included Accelerated Death Benefit rider provides additional peace of mind, allowing you to access a portion of your death benefit should you be diagnosed with a terminal illness with a life expectancy of 12 months or less.

Coverage Details

Death Benefit

The $250,000 death benefit is paid out to your designated beneficiaries upon your passing, as long as the policy is active and in good standing. This benefit is typically tax-free to the beneficiaries.

Term Length

This policy provides coverage for 20 years from the issue date, as long as premiums are paid on time. At the end of the term, coverage will cease unless you choose to convert to a permanent policy or renew (renewal would be at increased rates based on your age at that time).

Premium Structure

Your premium of $23 per month is guaranteed not to increase during the 20-year term. Premiums can be paid monthly, quarterly, semi-annually, or annually, with discounts available for annual payments.

Conversion Option

You have the option to convert this term policy to a permanent life insurance policy offered by Prudent Life at any time during the first 15 years of the term, without providing evidence of insurability. Conversion will result in a new premium based on your age at conversion.

Accelerated Death Benefit

If diagnosed with a terminal illness with a life expectancy of 12 months or less, you can access up to 50% of your death benefit (maximum $125,000) while still living. This advance is deducted from the final death benefit paid to beneficiaries.

Exclusions

Death benefit may not be paid in cases of suicide within the first two years of the policy, material misrepresentation on the application, or in certain other circumstances as detailed in the policy documents.

Frequently Asked Questions

What happens after the 20-year term ends? +

At the end of your 20-year term, your coverage will expire. You will have several options: 1) Convert to a permanent life insurance policy (if done within the conversion period), 2) Apply for a new term policy (subject to new underwriting and higher premiums based on your current age), or 3) Let the coverage end.

How quickly will the death benefit be paid? +

Once all required documentation is submitted, Prudent Life typically processes death benefit claims within 5-10 business days. The actual payment time can vary based on the complexity of the claim and the documentation provided.

Can I cancel my policy if I no longer need it? +

Yes, you can cancel your policy at any time by submitting a written request. If you cancel within the first 30 days (the free-look period), you'll receive a full refund of any premiums paid. After that period, you won't receive a refund of premiums paid, but no future premiums will be due.

Can I increase my coverage amount later? +

The death benefit amount cannot be increased on your existing policy. However, you can apply for an additional policy to increase your overall coverage. This would require a new application and underwriting process.

How do I name or change my beneficiaries? +

You can name or change your beneficiaries at any time by submitting a Beneficiary Change Request form. This form can be downloaded from our website or requested from our customer service team. The change becomes effective once processed by Prudent Life.
